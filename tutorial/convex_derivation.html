Convex Core — A Derivation from First Principles

How the allocation equation weq + wcvx + wdur = 1 and the volatility brake follow from one objective: maximizing long-horizon compound growth.
What this is — and isn't. Convex Core was originally authored from empirical evidence (the 2026-06-11 design spec, built from the day's backtests). This note shows that the same equation is what a first-principles argument recommends — so the theory and the empirics agree, which is the strongest position to be in. It is a principled justification, not a uniqueness proof: the derivation terminates in a small number of named design choices, and we mark each as rigorous or design choice so the reader can see exactly where judgment enters.

Step 0 — The objective: maximize geometric growth

Terminal wealth is multiplicative, so the correct objective is not the arithmetic mean return but the expected log-growth rate:

g = 𝔼[ ln(1 + R) ] long-run growth

To second order (a Taylor / Itô expansion in R):

g ≈ μ − ½ σ² μ = 𝔼[R], σ² = Var(R)
This one line drives the entire model. Variance is a direct subtraction from compound growth. Reducing σ² raises g even if it lowers the arithmetic mean μ — so "give up ≤1%/yr of arithmetic return to cut risk" is not risk-aversion, it is growth-optimal. rigorous

Drawdowns sharpen the point. Recovering from a loss of size d requires a gain of

d / (1 − d)  >  d −50% needs +100%

so recovery is convex in the loss, and negative skew (left-tail losses) makes the realized drag worse than the symmetric ½σ² term suggests. The quantity to minimize is therefore not total variance but specifically the left-tail / drawdown variance.

Step 1 — Why a structural allocation, not Markowitz

Classical mean–variance optimization solves for weights from (μ, Σ). It requires estimating expected returns μ — and the project's first empirical finding is that μ is not forecastable at useful precision on this universe (market-timing search returned negative; cross-sectional information coefficient ≈ 0.026). Under estimation error, mean–variance optimization is "error-maximizing" (Michaud): tiny μ-errors produce wildly unstable weights.

The principled response is to stop forecasting returns and allocate by role, under the one constraint that needs no estimate — full investment, no leverage:

Σi wi = 1 the budget constraint

That is the left-hand side of the equation. The only open question is what the roles are. rigorous, given the premise that μ is unforecastable

Step 2 — The three roles fall out of the risk decomposition

Exactly three functions are needed:

  1. Capture the one premium you believe in. The equity risk premium is the only large, persistent, theory-backed expected return → an equity core, weq.
  2. Hedge the left tail — but the tail has two regimes. The stock–bond correlation is regime-dependent: Treasuries rally in growth-scare / deflationary crashes (2008, 2020) but fall with equities in inflation / rate-shock selloffs (2022). One hedge cannot cover both. The tail-hedge therefore splits into two orthogonal sleeves:
    • Duration, wdur — carry/ballast that hedges the deflationary crash.
    • Crisis-convexity, wcvx — a long-volatility, trend-following / anti-beta sleeve that hedges the inflationary / momentum-crash regime duration misses.

Three terms, summing to one:

weq + wcvx + wdur = 1 wdur = 1 − weq − wcvx (the plug)
Why "convex." Trend-following has an option-like, convex payoff — Fung & Hsieh (2001) showed it replicates long lookback straddles. Its return curve versus the equity factor bends upward in the tails, injecting the positive co-skew that directly attacks the left-tail variance from Step 0. The sleeve is the portfolio's "long-gamma" leg.

design choice The principle forces "one premium sleeve + tail diversification"; choosing exactly two hedge sleeves for the two documented crash regimes is motivated by — not uniquely implied by — the argument. The 2022 attribution (convexity +4.5 while duration −1.6) is the empirical confirmation that the two sleeves are genuinely orthogonal.

Step 3 — The volatility brake from the Kelly criterion

For the equity core, the log-optimal (Kelly) fraction in a risky asset with excess return X ~ (μx, σx²) is

f* = μx / σx² Kelly / log-optimal sizing

Now apply the robust-estimation insight again. You cannot estimate μx, but the Sharpe ratio S = μx/σx is far more stable, and — crucially — volatility itself is forecastable (volatility clustering; the most robust regularity in daily returns). Substituting S:

f* = S / σx  ∝  1 / σx hold risk constant, not dollars

i.e. scale exposure inversely with realized volatility. Impose the no-leverage constraint (f ≤ the strategic weight) and you get a cap at 1 — exactly the brake:

eq_scale = min( 1,  σtarget / σ̂21d ) the volatility brake
The brake is the one piece of timing that survives, because it conditions only on σ̂ (estimable from volatility clustering) and never on μ (which is not). It "responds in days, with no classification machinery." rigorous, under "Sharpe more stable than the mean"

Step 4 — Freezing the parameters is implied, not bolted on

Because μ is unforecastable (Step 1), any in-sample optimization of the weights (weq, wcvx, σtarget, …) would fit noise and re-introduce exactly the instability the role-based design escaped. The growth-robust choice is to freeze the five parameters at structurally reasonable values and let the structure, not the calibration, carry the result — which makes the 21-year backtest out-of-sample by construction. "Zero fitting" is not a footnote; it is required by the same premise that produced the equation. rigorous, given the premise   the specific values 0.65 / 0.15 / 0.20 are a design choice

The defensible one-liner. The Convex Core equation is the form that maximizing expected log-growth implies once one accepts that expected returns are not forecastable but volatility is: a permanent premium-capturing core, a convex crisis hedge and a duration ballast for the two crash regimes, all sized by a Kelly-style volatility brake, with parameters frozen because optimizing them would re-create the very estimation error the structure exists to avoid.

Provenance & references

The mechanisms recombine well-published premia; the paper claims no novel strategy. To the authors' knowledge the specific three-sleeve, frozen-parameter formulation with the realized-volatility brake, as presented here, has not been previously published in this form.
